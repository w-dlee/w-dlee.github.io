User Performance in Consecutive Temporal Pointing: An Exploratory Study

Dawon Lee,

(Work in Progress)

Abstract

Users desire to watch video content with subtitles in various font sizes according to their viewing environment and personal preferences. However, since a \emph{chunk} of the subtitle, which represents a segment of the text corpus displayed on the screen at once, is typically constructed based on one specific font size, this may result in readability issues such as text truncation or awkward line breaks when attempting to accommodate different font sizes. Several methods have been proposed to address this challenge by reconstructing subtitle chunks based on font size variations. However, these methods operate solely on maximum character counts, neglecting synchronization with the content and resulting in misaligned text reveal moments. In response, we introduce \emph{OptiSub}, a fully automated system that optimizes subtitle segmentation to fit any font size while ensuring synchronization with the content. Our system utilizes speech pause timing between adjacent words in the audio for synchronization. Experimental results, including a user study comparing our system with previous research, demonstrate the effectiveness and practicality of OptiSub across diverse font sizes and input videos.
